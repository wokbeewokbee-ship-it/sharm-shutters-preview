# Sharm Shutters — Web Pages Build Brief

## Project Goal

Build the responsive website pages for **Sharm Shutters**, a professional roller-shutter and shading company in Sharm El Sheikh, Egypt.

The finished pages should present the company professionally, showcase its work and encourage visitors to contact the business or request a free site visit.

The complete page copy will be provided separately in the website content document. Use that document as the definitive content source.

## Scope

This task covers only:

- Building the website pages and their sections
- Creating the navigation and footer
- Applying a consistent visual design
- Adding the supplied content and approved images
- Making the pages responsive across desktop, tablet and mobile
- Creating reusable page components
- Connecting page buttons to the supplied contact, phone and WhatsApp destinations

Anything outside page design and construction will be handled separately by the project owner.

## Visual Direction

The design should feel:

- Contemporary
- Architectural
- Reliable
- Premium but approachable
- Appropriate for homes, stores, hotels and resorts in Sharm El Sheikh

Use:

- Strong real-project photography
- Clean geometric layouts
- Generous spacing
- Clear content hierarchy
- Large, direct CTAs
- Simple line icons where helpful
- A restrained palette based on the approved Sharm Shutters branding

If the final brand palette is not supplied, use a neutral temporary palette that can be replaced easily. Do not invent a permanent brand identity.

Avoid:

- Generic interior-design template styling
- Excessive animation
- Decorative clutter
- Fake ratings, counters or badges
- Fabricated testimonials
- Unsupported product specifications

## Responsive Requirements

Build mobile first and verify the pages at approximately:

- 320 px
- 360 px
- 390 px
- 768 px
- 1024 px
- 1440 px

Requirements:

- No horizontal overflow
- Readable text without zooming
- Minimum 44 × 44 px interactive targets
- Comfortable spacing between controls
- Responsive project grids and galleries
- Mobile-friendly dropdown navigation
- Sticky WhatsApp button that does not cover content
- Forms that fit naturally on small screens
- Images that remain sharp and properly cropped

## Accessibility

- Use semantic headings and page landmarks
- One primary H1 per page
- Maintain logical heading order
- Provide visible keyboard focus
- Ensure keyboard access to menus, filters and accordions
- Use persistent form labels
- Maintain strong text/background contrast
- Add meaningful alt text to informative images
- Treat decorative images as decorative
- Avoid autoplay audio or video
- Respect reduced-motion preferences

## Interaction Guidelines

- Keep animations subtle and fast
- Give buttons clear hover, focus and active states
- Make project filters easy to understand on mobile
- Show useful empty states if a project category has no entries
- Do not create dead buttons or placeholder links in the final handoff
- Ensure CTA wording remains consistent with the supplied content

## Content Rules

- Use the supplied content document without changing verified business facts
- Do not invent services, materials, product features or warranties
- Do not invent testimonials, reviews, certifications or experience numbers
- Keep any item marked `[CONFIRM]` hidden until approved
- Do not publish customer or property logos without permission
- Do not add pages outside the approved sitemap

## Final Page-Build Checklist

- All approved pages and Solution subpages are built
- Main navigation and mobile navigation work correctly
- Every Solution card links to the correct page
- Project filters and project details work
- All supplied content is placed in the correct sections
- Images are mapped to the correct categories
- CTAs use the correct phone, WhatsApp or contact destination
- No unsupported claims or `[CONFIRM]` placeholders are visible
- Layouts work across mobile, tablet and desktop
- Menus, accordions, filters, galleries and forms are keyboard accessible
- No broken links, missing images, overflow or overlapping elements remain
- Reusable components are organized clearly for future updates

## Definition of Done

The task is complete when all pages in the approved sitemap are built using the supplied content and approved images; the design is consistent and responsive; navigation, project filters, accordions and CTAs work; no unapproved claims or extra pages appear; and the page files/components are ready for the project owner to integrate and complete the remaining website setup independently.
